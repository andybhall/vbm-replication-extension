## Study Details

### 1. Tregellas et al. (2007)
**Title**: Gray matter volume differences and the effects of smoking on gray matter in schizophrenia
**PMID**: [17890058](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/17890058/)
**Sample**: 32 SZ (14 smokers, 18 non-smokers) + 32 HC
**Method**: VBM
**Key finding**: Smokers with SZ had GREATER lateral prefrontal and STG gray matter vs non-smokers (potential protective effect)
**Symptom data**: To be verified

### 2. Van Haren et al. (2010)
**Title**: Cigarette smoking and progressive brain volume loss in schizophrenia
**PMID**: [20227855](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/20227855/)
**Sample**: Longitudinal cohort
**Method**: Volumetric (total brain, GM, WM, ventricles)
**Key finding**: Heavy smoking may contribute to excessive GM loss over time in SZ
**Symptom data**: PANSS (to be verified)

### 3. Schneider et al. (2014)
**Title**: Smoking status as a potential confounder in the study of brain structure in schizophrenia
**PMID**: [24373929](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/24373929/)
**Sample**: 112 SZ (53 smokers, 59 non-smokers) + 77 HC non-smokers
**Method**: Automated atlas-based segmentation
**Key finding**: Smoker SZ had LOWER hippocampal and DLPFC volumes (2.2-2.8% reduction)
**Symptom data**: Yes (to be verified)

### 4. Jørgensen et al. (2015)
**Title**: Cigarette smoking is associated with thinner cingulate and insular cortices in patients with severe mental illness
**PMID**: [25672482](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/25672482/)
**Sample**: SZ/schizoaffective + bipolar + HC
**Method**: FreeSurfer cortical thickness
**Key finding**: Smokers showed thinner left rostral ACC and insula (small effect ~0.05mm)
**Symptom data**: To be verified

### 5. Yu et al. (2018)
**Title**: Additive effect of cigarette smoking on gray matter abnormalities in schizophrenia
**PMC**: [PMC5890451](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC5890451/)
**Sample**: 4 groups (SZ smokers, SZ non-smokers, HC smokers, HC non-smokers)
**Method**: VBM
**Key finding**: Negative additive effects in left prefrontal cortex; decrease associated with pack-years and symptom severity
**Symptom data**: Yes - PANSS positive and negative

### 6. Ringin et al. (2022)
**Title**: The impact of smoking status on cognition and brain morphology in schizophrenia spectrum disorders
**PMID**: [33443010](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/33443010/)
**Sample**: 82 HC + 201 SSD with T1 scans (from larger cohort of 299 HC + 455 SSD)
**Method**: Cortical volume, thickness, surface area
**Key finding**: Examined smoking × diagnosis interaction on cognition and brain morphology
**Symptom data**: Yes
