## Key Findings by Brain Region

### Prefrontal Cortex (DLPFC, lateral PFC)

| Study | Finding | Direction |
|-------|---------|-----------|
| Tregellas 2007 | Smokers with SZ had GREATER lateral PFC GM | ↑ in smokers |
| Schneider 2014 | Smokers with SZ had LOWER DLPFC volume (~2.5%) | ↓ in smokers |
| Yokoyama 2018 | Additive effect: SZ + smoking → reduced left PFC | ↓ in smokers |
| Qiu 2024 | Tobacco → positive GMV correlation in PFC | ↑ with tobacco |

**Summary**: Mixed findings. Early study (Tregellas) suggested potential protective effect; later studies show reduction.

### Hippocampus

| Study | Finding | Direction |
|-------|---------|-----------|
| Schneider 2014 | Smokers with SZ had LOWER hippocampal volume (~2.2%) | ↓ in smokers |
| Qiu 2024 | Negative GMV correlation with tobacco use | ↓ with tobacco |
| Musket 2026 | Focus on hippocampus in SZ+SUD | TBD |

**Summary**: Consistent reduction in hippocampal volume with smoking.

### Anterior Cingulate Cortex (ACC)

| Study | Finding | Direction |
|-------|---------|-----------|
| Jørgensen 2015 | Smokers with SZ had thinner left rostral ACC | ↓ in smokers |
| Qiu 2024 | Part of DMN abnormalities with tobacco | ↓ with tobacco |

**Summary**: Consistent reduction in ACC thickness/volume.

### Insula

| Study | Finding | Direction |
|-------|---------|-----------|
| Jørgensen 2015 | Smokers with SZ had thinner left insula | ↓ in smokers |
| Qiu 2024 | Negative GMV correlation in insula | ↓ with tobacco |

**Summary**: Consistent reduction in insula.

### Temporal Cortex (STG)

| Study | Finding | Direction |
|-------|---------|-----------|
| Tregellas 2007 | Smokers with SZ had GREATER right STG GM | ↑ in smokers |

**Summary**: Only one study; suggests possible preservation.

### Global Measures

| Study | Finding |
|-------|---------|
| Van Haren 2010 | Smoking did NOT explain progressive GM loss; heavy smoking (>25 CPD) may accelerate loss |
| Ringin 2022 | HC smokers: widespread reductions; SSD smokers: attenuated effect (interaction) |

## Diagnosis × Smoking Interactions

| Study | Interaction Found? | Interpretation |
|-------|-------------------|----------------|
| Tregellas 2007 | Yes | Smoking moderated GM deficits (potential protection) |
| Van Haren 2010 | No | No significant interaction |
| Schneider 2014 | Yes (partial) | Non-smoker SZ ≈ HC; smoker SZ < HC |
| Jørgensen 2015 | Yes | Smoking effects specific to patients |
| Yokoyama 2018 | No | Additive (not interactive) effects |
| Ringin 2022 | Yes | SSD showed attenuated smoking effect vs. HC |
| Qiu 2024 | Yes | Tobacco × SZ interaction in PFC-symptom correlation |
| Musket 2026 | No | No explicit interaction reported |

**Summary**: 5/8 studies report some form of interaction, but direction varies.

## Methodological Notes

### Strengths
- Mix of VBM and surface-based (FreeSurfer) approaches
- Several multi-site studies with large samples
- Both global and regional measures

### Limitations
- Cross-sectional designs (except Van Haren 2010)
- Varying smoking definitions
- No standardized pack-year thresholds
- No psychological flexibility measures
- DTI excluded (see rationale in PCC_framework.md)

## Convergent Brain Regions (for Psychological Flexibility Hypothesis)

Regions showing consistent smoking-related effects that OVERLAP with psychological flexibility fMRI findings:

| Region | sMRI Smoking Effect | Psychological Flexibility (fMRI) |
|--------|---------------------|----------------------------------|
| **Prefrontal (DLPFC)** | ↓ in most studies | Altered frontoparietal connectivity |
| **ACC** | ↓ in smokers | ACT intervention target (Smallwood 2016) |
| **Insula** | ↓ in smokers | ACT intervention target (Smallwood 2016) |
| **Putamen/striatum** | ↓ (Qiu 2024) | Lower flexibility → altered connectivity |

This regional overlap supports the theoretical justification for examining psychological flexibility in relation to brain structure.
